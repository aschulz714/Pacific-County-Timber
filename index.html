Method & sources

How this was made.

  1. Pacific County Department of Public Works Taxlot (Parcels) shapefile.
  2. Filtered in QGIS 3.14 to department code 88 — Designated Forest Land.
  3. Exported to GeoJSON, simplified line intersections in mapshaper.org, stored as TopoJSON.
  4. Rendered with Mapbox GL JS v3 against a custom Mapbox Studio basemap.
  5. Owner tiers computed by aggregating each parcel's CURRENT_O string against the dataset's total acres for that owner. Thresholds: institutional > 1,000 ac; mid-tier 100–1,000; small-holder < 100.
  6. Regional port markers are reference points — Grays Harbor, Willapa Harbor, Longview — at approximate harbor centers, accurate to ~1km. Not an exhaustive shipping-infrastructure layer.
  7. All statistics on this page are computed live from the loaded TopoJSON — no precomputation.

Notes

What the map shows.

Pacific County sits on Washington's southwest coast and is one of the most timber-heavy counties in the state. The dominant pattern, visible at a glance, is concentrated ownership: Weyerhaeuser holds roughly a third of the forest acres outright; Rayonier is a distant second; the top ten owners between them hold the majority. The smaller, brighter-red parcels under "Price / acre" are the highest-value parcels per acre — typically closer to roads or with structures.

This is a 2026 refresh of a 2021 GIS class project. The original GIS pipeline is preserved. What's new in 2026: live-computed statistics, an interactive owner panel that highlights individual holdings on the map, a metric toggle between price-per-acre / total acres / assessed value, a minimum-size filter, an owner-tier filter framed for acquisition screening (institutional / mid-tier / small-holder), regional port markers for haul-distance context, a key-findings synthesis below the map, and a Mapbox GL JS v2 → v3 upgrade with hover and selection states reimplemented.

Limits to acknowledge: the parcel data is a 2021 snapshot, not a live feed. Timber productivity (site index, age class, species) and regulatory overlays (Marbled Murrelet habitat, riparian buffers) are not in this dataset and would require WA DNR / WDFW layers to add. Mill locations are intentionally not shown — adding them without verified coordinates would weaken rather than strengthen the analysis.
